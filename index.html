Sándor Kisfaludi‑Bak

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Science at Aalto University. Previously I was a postdoc at the Institute for Theoretical Studies at ETH Zürich, and before that, at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics.

My main interest lies in exact and approximation algorithms and their hardness in computational geometry.

Among other things, my research is focused on the following topics:

  • Non-Euclidean (especially hyperbolic) computational geometry
  • Optimization problems on point sets and objects in Euclidean and non-Euclidean spaces, e.g. the traveling salesman and Steiner tree
  • Parameterized, exact, and approximation algorithms on planar graphs and beyond
  • Structure and algorithmic properties of geometric intersection graphs
